PCM Integration for HVAC Load Reduction (EnergyPlus)

Team project analyzing how Phase Change Materials (PCM) can reduce cooling load and peak demand when integrated into the building thermal envelope. Performance was evaluated using EnergyPlus building simulations.

EnergyPlus PCM Cooling Load Peak Demand Team Project

Objective

Evaluate PCM-based thermal storage within the building envelope and quantify its impact on cooling load, peak demand, and annual energy consumption using EnergyPlus simulations.

My Role

  • Built and configured building energy simulation models using EnergyPlus.
  • Supported PCM modeling integration (envelope thermal behavior) and scenario setup.
  • Performed simulation runs and post-processing for HVAC load and annual energy comparisons.
  • Interpreted results and contributed to team presentation/reporting.

Tools

  • EnergyPlus (building energy simulation)
  • Data analysis + reporting (figures/tables)

Key Results

  • Annual energy reduction reported: ~346 kWh (PCM case vs baseline).
  • PCM helps reduce temperature swings and can lower cooling load under suitable conditions.
  • Benefits depend on climate, PCM properties, and placement within the envelope.

Design Highlights

  • Baseline vs PCM scenario comparison
  • Cooling load + peak demand evaluation
  • Annual energy and performance interpretation

Baseline vs PCM Summary

Aspect Baseline PCM-Enhanced
Thermal Behavior Standard envelope response Latent heat buffering reduces temperature swings
Cooling Load Higher (no storage) Lower / shifted load depending on conditions
Peak Demand Higher peak ramps Potential peak reduction via thermal storage
Annual Energy Baseline reference Reported reduction: ~346 kWh

Note: Results depend strongly on PCM properties, location in the envelope, and climate/operating conditions.
