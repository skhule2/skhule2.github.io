Hybrid Solar & Wind Assisted EV Charging System

Team project (4 members) completed in 2024. Designed and built a hybrid renewable charging prototype combining solar PV and wind energy harvesting to support EV battery charging via an auxiliary battery system.

SolidWorks CAD Prototype Build Solar PV Wind Turbine Testing
Project Overview

Developed a hybrid renewable system to generate electrical energy from solar and wind inputs. The setup uses a turbine-driven alternator and a solar panel with a charge controller to charge an auxiliary battery, reducing dependence on conventional charging and demonstrating feasibility for range extension / auxiliary power support.

MY Role
  • CAD Modeling: designed the full CAD model independently (SolidWorks).
  • Design Calculations: supported system calculations and sizing.
  • Fabrication: built the prototype collaboratively with the team.
  • Electrical Wiring: assisted with wiring and connections.
Design Highlights
  • Hybrid input: solar PV + wind turbine/alternator.
  • Energy stored in an auxiliary battery for practical use.
  • Focus on manufacturable design + testable prototype.
Results & Observations

The system successfully charged the vehicle battery when the alternator was in motion, while the solar panel continuously provided charging during daytime operation. Battery voltage increased proportionally with alternator RPM, confirming effective mechanical-to-electrical energy conversion. The hybrid approach demonstrated improved overall charging efficiency compared to a single-source system. Utilized aerodynamic air pressure (vehicle motion) to generate supplemental electrical energy. Reduced dependency on conventional grid charging. Concept supports eco-friendly vehicle range extension.

Challenges
  • Mechanical alignment & coupling between turbine shaft and alternator.
  • Electrical regulation and safe connections during testing.
  • Balancing stability, efficiency, and real-world constraints.
