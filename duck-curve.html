Solar Duck Curve Modeling & Ramp Mitigation

MATLAB-based modeling of demand curve behavior and evaluation of battery + demand response strategies to reduce evening ramp and improve grid stability.

Project Summary

This project analyzed the “duck curve” problem caused by increasing solar generation, where midday net demand dips and evening net demand ramps quickly. I focused on modeling the demand curve behavior and evaluating mitigation using demand response and battery storage strategies.

MATLAB Demand Response Battery Dispatch Grid Integration

My Role

  • Modeled the demand curve behavior and generated key curves in MATLAB.
  • Performed demand response analysis to reduce peak ramp impact.
  • Performed battery analysis/dispatch logic to shift energy and smooth ramp.
  • Compiled plots/outputs used in the final report.

Tools

  • MATLAB (curve modeling + analysis)
  • Data plotting + interpretation
  • Report + engineering documentation

Method (High-Level)

  • Built the demand curve model and baseline net demand behavior.
  • Applied demand response assumptions to reduce critical peak load.
  • Applied battery charge/discharge dispatch to shift midday excess into evening ramp.
  • Compared baseline vs mitigated net demand curves.

Results / Key Takeaways

  • Demonstrated reduced evening ramp using storage dispatch.
  • Showed how demand response reduces peak stress periods.
  • Highlighted grid stability benefits from combined DR + BESS.
