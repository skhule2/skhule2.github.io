Double-Sided Cooling & Wire-Bondless SiC Power Modules for Solar Inverters

Research project for my Fundamentals of Semiconductor Packaging course focused on how advanced module packaging improves thermal performance, reduces parasitic inductance, and increases reliability for SiC-based power modules in solar inverters.

Double-Sided Cooling Wire-Bondless Solar Inverters Thermal + Reliability

Objective

Analyze advanced semiconductor packaging techniques that enhance the thermal, electrical, and reliability performance of SiC-based power modules used in solar inverters.

What I did

  • Reviewed literature on double-sided cooling and wire-bondless module designs.
  • Compared performance drivers: thermal resistance (RthJC), parasitic inductance, and reliability failure modes.
  • Summarized packaging materials used in high-performance modules (Ag sinter, DBC/AMB, TIMs).
  • Presented practical impact on solar inverter efficiency, power density, and lifetime.

Key Takeaways

  • Double-sided cooling removes heat from both sides of the die, lowering junction temperature and thermal resistance.
  • Wire-bondless interconnects reduce inductance, overshoot, and bond-wire fatigue issues.
  • Combined approach supports compact, efficient, high-reliability solar inverters.

Highlighted Metrics (from literature)

  • Junction temperature reduction: ~25–45°C vs single-sided cooled modules (same load).
  • Parasitic inductance: ~4–5 nH wire-bondless vs ~15–20 nH conventional.
  • Reliability: up to ~3× longer power-cycling lifetime (reported).

Traditional vs Advanced Packaging (Summary)

Feature Traditional Wire-Bonded Advanced (DSC + Wire-Bondless)
Cooling Single-sided (bottom only) Double-sided (top + bottom)
Interconnect Al bond wires Planar Cu pillars / sintered joints
Thermal Resistance (RthJC) Higher Lower
Stray Inductance Higher (~15–20 nH) Lower (~4–5 nH)
Reliability Bond-wire lift-off + fatigue No bond wires → improved lifetime

Note: Values are literature-reported ranges used in the presentation and may vary by module design and test conditions.
